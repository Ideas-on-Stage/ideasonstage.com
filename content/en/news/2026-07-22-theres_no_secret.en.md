Whenever someone says, “Here’s the secret to…” – I stop listening.

The secret to losing weight.
The secret to becoming a millionaire.
The secret to mastering public speaking.

There’s no secret. The way to get good at anything is simple: put in thousands of hours.

Ask a chef, “What’s the secret to this recipe?” They’ll likely say, “I’ve been a chef for twenty years. That’s the secret.”

Talking about secrets dismisses the work. Mastery comes from time and effort. From failing and trying again.

So if you want to become a great presenter, don’t look for secrets. Get on stage. Practise. Learn. Repeat. The more you do it, the better you’ll get.

It’s not magic – it’s just work.
